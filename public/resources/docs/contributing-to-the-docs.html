Contributing to the Docs

We welcome contributions to the Beaver Notes documentation! Whether you are a developer or a non-coder, there are multiple ways to contribute and help us improve our documentation to benefit the entire community.

Writing and Editing

If you have excellent writing skills and want to help us improve the clarity and accuracy of the documentation, we'd love to have your assistance. You can:

  • Identify areas that need improvement or expansion.
  • Suggest edits and revisions to existing content.
  • Write new sections or topics to cover additional aspects of Beaver Notes.

Feel free to make a copy of the repository, edit the files, and submit a pull request for our review. Your contributions will be invaluable in ensuring that the documentation remains comprehensive and user-friendly.

Reporting Issues

Found a bug, an error, or something that needs clarification in the documentation? Let us know! By submitting an issue on GitHub, you help us identify areas that require attention, allowing us to address them promptly.

Providing Feedback

Your feedback is crucial in making the documentation more accessible and informative. Whether you are a seasoned user or just starting with Beaver Notes, your insights are valuable. Share your thoughts, suggestions, and ideas with us on our social media platforms or through email at danielerolli@proton.me

Non-Coding Contributions

Even if you don't have coding skills, you can still make a significant impact on the documentation. You can help by:

  • Reporting typos or grammatical errors.
  • Testing and verifying the instructions in the documentation.
  • Suggesting improvements to the overall structure and organization of the content.

Coding Contributions

For those with coding skills, contributing to the documentation is just a few steps away. To get started, you'll need the following:

  • Node.js (v16 or higher)
  • Yarn
  • Git

Follow these steps to contribute:

1. Fork the Beaver Notes Website repository on GitHub.

2. Clone your forked repository to your local machine using git.

git clone https://github.com/Daniele-rolli/beaver-website

3. Change into the project's directory.

cd beaver-website

4. Install the required dependecies

yarn install

    5. Start the local development server to preview your changes.

    yarn dev

    Once you're satisfied with your contributions, submit a pull request, and our team will review it promptly.

    Remember, every contribution, no matter how big or small, is appreciated and valued. Together, we can create a robust and comprehensive resource that empowers the entire Beaver Notes community.

    We look forward to collaborating with you to make Beaver Notes even better!
